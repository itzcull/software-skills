# Domains in DDD

In Domain-Driven Design (DDD), a domain represents the specific knowledge, activities, and concerns related to a particular business or application context.

## Characteristics of a Domain

A domain has several key characteristics:
- [Entities](entity.md): Key things that exist within the domain
- **Rules**: Governing behavior of entities
- **Processes**: Activities carried out within the domain
- **Complexity and size**: Ranges from simple to complex
- **Domain boundaries**: Separating one domain from another

## Types of Domains

### Core Domain
- Represents the primary area of focus for a business
- Includes key business processes that differentiate the organization
- Where the business aims to excel and gain competitive advantage

### Strategic Domains
- Areas with significant impact on organizational success
- Involve key business strategies
- Can influence the direction of the entire business

### Subdomains
- Smaller subsets of a domain
- More detailed breakdown of domain components

## Identifying and Defining Domains

Domains are created through the practice of [domain modeling](domain-model.md).

## Example Context

In an eCommerce system:
- Overall domain: "Online Retail"
- Core Domain: Online Retail
- Strategic Domains: 
  - Personalization
  - Recommendations
  - Supply Chain Optimization
  - Market Entry
