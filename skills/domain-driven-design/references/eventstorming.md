# EventStorming in Domain-Driven Design

## Definition

EventStorming is a collaborative workshop-based technique that helps teams gain a deeper understanding of complex business domains. It brings together stakeholders, IT professionals, developers, domain experts, business analysts, designers, and other relevant participants to explore and map out a domain's processes and interactions.

## Key Characteristics

- Collaborative approach to understanding complex domains
- Uses sticky notes of different colors to represent various domain elements
- Facilitates communication across different teams and roles

## Types of EventStorming

1. Big Picture EventStorming
2. Process Modeling
3. Software Design
4. People Experience
5. Retrospective Analysis

## Workshop Process

1. Set clear goals for the session
2. Choose workshop mode (in-person or virtual)
3. Create events using orange sticky notes
4. Identify pivotal events and timeline points
5. Order events chronologically
6. Add roles (yellow stickies)
7. Add commands (blue stickies)
8. Capture questions and pain points (red stickies)

## Sticky Note Color System

- **Orange**: Domain events
- **Yellow**: Actors/Roles
- **Blue**: Commands
- **Red**: Questions and pain points
- **Purple**: Business processes/logic
- **Pink**: External systems
- **Green**: Views/read models

## Benefits

- Develops a shared [ubiquitous language](./ubiquitous-language.md)
- Improves team communication
- Visualizes collective understanding
- Facilitates domain exploration
- Supports onboarding of new team members
- Identifies [bounded contexts](./bounded-context.md)

## Best Practices

- Invite a neutral facilitator
- Encourage open dialogue
- Capture all perspectives
- Focus on collaborative learning
- Include diverse stakeholders
- Allow sufficient time for the process
