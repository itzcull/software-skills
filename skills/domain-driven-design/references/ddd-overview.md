# Domain Driven Design (DDD) Overview

## Definition

Domain Driven Design (DDD) is "a software design strategy intended to take complex domains (real-world problems) and simplify them into an extensible and maintainable software solution."

## Origin

- Introduced by Eric Evans in 2003
- Focuses on complex real-world problem solving through software design

## Benefits

- Simplifies complex domains
- Creates extensible software solutions
- Improves maintainability
- Provides structured approach to modeling complex systems

## When to Use DDD

DDD is most beneficial when:
- Working with complex business domains
- Multiple stakeholders have different perspectives on the domain
- The domain logic is substantial and sophisticated
- The project will have a long lifecycle
